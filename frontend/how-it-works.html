Common Questions

Is my vote really secret? expand_more
Yes. Civic Ledger uses cryptographic blinding. The authority can verify that you are a registered voter who has cast one ballot, but they have no technical way to link your identity to the specific choices inside that ballot.
What if I lose my private key? expand_more
During registration, you are provided with a recovery phrase. If both are lost, you must visit a local verification center in person to reset your biometric profile.
Can I change my vote after submitting? expand_more
No. To maintain the integrity of the ledger, once a block is confirmed, it is permanent. This prevents "vote manipulation" and ensures the count is final.
